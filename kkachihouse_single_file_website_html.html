Our Story — From Kkachinae (1983) to the UK

KKachihouse is proudly associated with Kkachinae, established in 1983 in Korea. We carry forward that heritage of honest flavours and street‑side cooking, serving playful classics like corndogs, kimbap, and tteokbokki to UK streets, markets, and festivals.

We source UK ingredients where possible and prepare everything fresh at the truck. Whether you’re new to Korean food or a lifelong fan, our menu balances comfort and spice, crunch and chew — the true spirit of Seoul street food.

Why “KKachi”?

In Korean, kkachi means magpie — a symbol of good news. We hope our food brings a little celebration to your day.
